01

Bathing on the quays of the Saône

On the quays of the Saône, opposite the 24-column courthouse in Lyon, a baths project. The aim is to recreate a dialogue in a space that is now considered to be a passageway, a slab that has lost all connection with the river. A dialogue between the space and the water, between the inhabitants and the Saône, but also between the surface and the underground. How can we bring light into the baths without damaging the public space above ground? How can the privacy of bathers be protected? What is the relationship between the river and the city?

02

The five homes

Situated in the Croix-Rousse district, adjacent to a high-rise flat block and an Ehpad, a five-unit housing project. The context poses challenges in terms of views and sunlight. At the heart of the plot is a majestic cedar tree. Opposite to the cutting of the cedar, it was a central element of the project. The housing programme raises many questions about the relationship with communal spaces, the importance given to living areas, and the place given to appropriation but also to privacy

03

Designing with what exists

The project is located in Vaise, a district of Lyon in the 9th arrondissement. A historically industrial district, we can still see traces of this, as is the case with our project site: a former factory. After interviewing local residents, we realised that this is an area where two populations meet: one, historically more modest and working-class, while the second, a more recent arrival, is more affluent. The challenge will be to bring them together here.As for the old factory, it has many qualities, such as a majestic central hall covered with sheds.

Personal productions

SHORT FILM

During my first two years at ENSAL, I was able to take part in the short film competition organised by the school's film association. This gave me the opportunity to make two short films with Gaïa Bergeret.
Both were great experiences for us. It was an opportunity for us to experiment with a new creative medium and produce a project that we're proud of. We're both passionate about film, and directing allows us to put our ideas into images and, year on year, learn more about the technical aspects.
We're also learning to be adaptable, open to discovery and humble about our abilities. We also get involved because of the creative freedom we are given. The experience of creative research is very stimulating and opens up new horizons. It sharpens our eye for shots, light and rhythm. On a personal level, the experience of coordinating a group of people and a schedule teaches us more about team management, time management and organisation. It also forces us to feel legitimate, to dare to take the lead.

SCENOGRAPHY

I'm in charge of the set design for the school's music association. I'm in charge of the lighting, decorations, etc., making the space their own for the musicians.

PICTORAL PROJECT

As part of a three-week pictorial project based on the collections of the Musée des Beaux-Arts de Lyon, I chose to work on the gaze. My subject focused on the figures staring at the viewer in paintings of large scenes. I see these gazes as a direct link with a person from another time, a search for contact. But what I also find very touching is this feeling of escape from their own painting, an exteriority to their own scene.
